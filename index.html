Felicity
Boutique

Website creation

A project created for a local buisness

I chose to design and create a website for Felicity's Boutique, a local store owned by my wifeâ€™s friend; to fulfill requirements for a college course. The site, design, logo, and asset generation was completed within a 3 week period. The end goal of the website was to generate interest and drive individuals into the store utilizing a seasonal catalog and a landing page for the business, based on search engine results.
The design is to be clean and light with small splashes of color to create a nice contrast. I really wanted to make the contact information, address and physical map easy to find. To accomplish these goals within the time frame, I decided to use the Bootstrap framework allowing me to generate a fully responsive design utilizing its grid and various other parts.
I learned a lot about Bootstrap during this time period and utilized a lot of its offerings.

Photography portfolio

A portfolio website for a local photographer, focusing on their work

I got to a point that I could leverage all of my knowledge of JavaScript, CSS, html and bootstrap. I used bootstraps carousel functions for a fully responsive slideshow/gallery hybrid.

The design was created with a pre-existing logo, graphics and water mark. I ran into some challenges working with PNG originals and rescaleability. To overcome this issue I re-made the logo in illustrator and added some interactivity with the hover effects.

Durango
Bike Co.

Website Re-design

Web Re-design of a small bike company out of Durango Colorado

Durango Bike Co. is a boutique bike manufacturer out of Durango Colorado. Their primary audience is adults, 25-45 years old with household incomes greater than $80,000 a year. Durango Bike Co. prides itself in being authentic and mindful. Bikes are hand built in the USA, with American made parts/companies sourced wherever possible. Durango Bike Co. is mindful in that it creates its bikes using 100% solar power. This positions Durango Bike Co. well within the niche bike market. According to IMBA 40 million Americans mountain bike each year (IMBA), with enthusiasts driving the 6.1 Billion dollar bicycle industry (NBDA).
